System Analyst Internship · 2026

From Screen to System.

My first AI-assisted case study in understanding how data moves from storage to interface.

I worked beside a System Engineer—shaping screen structures, end-to-end flows, and the behavior users need to see.

Case study Smart Asset Management

01 · The problem

One asset system. Too many realities.

Assets, materials, sites, documents, and rental work followed different rules. The challenge was making them feel like one system.

4 Business units with different responsibilities
30 Use cases mapped across the platform
9 Stages from request to return and billing

02 · My role

Working beside an SE.

I translated business needs into interfaces and flows, then revised them when technical constraints exposed gaps in my thinking.

What I owned

Screen structure & user flow

Organized information, mapped actions, and explained how each step should behave.

What the SE challenged

Data, jobs & edge cases

Storage choices, recurring checks, permissions, and failure states had to work beyond the UI.

How AI helped

Faster exploration

AI accelerated documentation, flow exploration, and prototype production—not the final decisions.

The flow separated proposal, approval, customer confirmation, real reservation, delivery, and return.

Operational detail matters.

A return is not one button. It needs rounds, evidence, inspection, quantities, and exception states.

04 · Hardest challenge

Master Asset was not one thing.

A vehicle, a drill, a bag of cement, and an expiring permit cannot share the same lifecycle just because they appear in one menu.

01

Durable asset

Track each serialized unit, location, status, QR, movement, and maintenance.

02

Consumable material

Track quantity by lot, cost, FIFO movement, and remaining balance.

03

Renewal document

Keep dated records and history—never overwrite the previous evidence.

05 · The key decision

Do not bury operational rules in JSON.

Fields used for daily checks, reporting, warnings, and locks needed to remain explicit and easy to query.

Flexible—but hidden

06 · Designing to last

A system that should survive for years.

I prepared Master Asset so future reminders and usage locks could be added without restructuring its core data.

One entry, different records

Durable items create serialized assets; consumables create lot-based stock movement.

Data returned as decisions

Site users see status, deadlines, ownership, and the next action—not database structure.

The screen is only the last layer.

01

Data decisions shape the interface.

A field is not only something users type. It determines what the system can search, calculate, and enforce.

02

Background behavior is still UX.

Reminders and locks must work even when nobody has opened the screen.

03

Technical feedback changed my design.

SE challenges pushed me beyond a screen-first mindset toward system-aware UX/UI.

AI disclosure

AI accelerated documentation, flow exploration, and prototype production. I defined the requirements, made design decisions, validated outputs with the System Engineer, and revised them against real constraints.
